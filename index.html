Let's talk about what we're not talking about

There are about
3.3 million Filipinos who live with a depressive disorder

According to the Department of Health, suicide rates are 2.5 males and 1.7 females for every 100,000 persons. The World Health Organization (WHO) also reported over 2,000 cases of suicide from 2000 to 2012, most of which involved people aged 15 to 29 years old.

There is a scarcity of mental health professionals in the Philippines, with only a little over 500 practicing psychiatrists. The ratio presented is lower than other countries with similar income levels such as Malaysia (1.27 per 100,000) and Indonesia (0.3 per 100,000). Furthermore, access to mental health services is not equally distributed across the country, as most psychiatrists work in for-profit or private sectors in larger urban cities such as Metro Manila.

There is a ratio of only 0.52 psychiatrists per 100,000 persons

In 2018, President Rodrigo Duterte signed the landmark Mental Health Act

This was the first mental health act legislation in the Philippines. It outlines a framework for the integration and implementation of optimal mental health care, and protects the rights of people with mental health conditions, their family members and industry professionals.
